03_hypothesis_test.R 
# в”Җв”Җ Two-Sample t-Test: Units 7вҖ“8 в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җ

# Standard Error
SE <- sqrt((s1^2 / n1) + (s2^2 / n2))
cat("Standard Error (SE):", round(SE, 4), "\n")

# Test Statistic
diff   <- xbar1 - xbar2
t_stat <- diff / SE
cat("Test Statistic (t):", round(t_stat, 4), "\n")

# Degrees of Freedom (WelchвҖ“Satterthwaite)
num <- (s1^2/n1 + s2^2/n2)^2
den <- ((s1^2/n1)^2/(n1-1)) + ((s2^2/n2)^2/(n2-1))
df  <- num / den
cat("Degrees of Freedom:", round(df, 2), "\n")

# p-value (two-tailed)
p_val <- 2 * pt(-abs(t_stat), df = df)
cat("p-value:", round(p_val, 6), "\n")
cat("Decision:", if(p_val < 0.05) "Reject HвӮҖ вң“" else "Fail to Reject HвӮҖ", "\n")

Statistical Conclusion

With t = 4.40, df вүҲ 21, and p вүҲ 0.00024, we reject HвӮҖ at the Оұ = 0.05 significance level. There is strong statistical evidence that mean perceived productivity differs between students who study with music and those who study in silence at Doral Academy.

The 95% confidence interval (0.799, 2.229) does not include zero, further supporting our conclusion. We are 95% confident that music students rate their productivity between 0.799 and 2.229 points higher on a 5-point scale than silence students.

Because this is an observational study, we cannot establish causation. The association may be influenced by confounding variables such as student motivation, academic ability, or study environment preferences.
